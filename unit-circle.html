What is a unit circle?

A unit circle is a circle with a radius of 1. The unit circle has its center in the origin of a graph. Because the radius is one, a person can measure sin and cos directly from the graph. This is important in trigonometry because it is a mixture of both trigonometry and graphing, which helps a lot with the constructions, and helps with visualizing the unit circle when it is graphed.
